<p class="wp-block-paragraph">PHP Errors are defects or difficulties that arise when a script is being executed in PHP. The PHP interpreter produces an errors message to explain what went wrong when it encounters an error. Developers can find and address problems in their code with the aid of these error messages. </p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="1024" height="683" src="/images/blog/php-errors.webp" alt="PHP Errors" class="wp-image-683" style="width:613px;height:auto"  sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></figure>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>PHP Errors fall into several categories and can happen for a number of reasons.</strong></h3>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>1. Syntax Errors:</strong></p>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Description:</strong> These&nbsp;issues&nbsp;arise&nbsp;from&nbsp;syntactic&nbsp;flaws&nbsp;in&nbsp;PHP&nbsp;code.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>How&nbsp;to&nbsp;Address&nbsp;It: </strong>Check&nbsp;the&nbsp;code&nbsp;surrounding&nbsp;the&nbsp;line&nbsp;that&nbsp;was&nbsp;reported&nbsp;and&nbsp;correct&nbsp;any&nbsp;syntax&nbsp;errors. Parentheses,&nbsp;missing&nbsp;semicolons,&nbsp;and&nbsp;improperly&nbsp;nested&nbsp;structures&nbsp;are&nbsp;common&nbsp;mistakes.</p>
</div>
</div>



<div style="height:34px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="512" height="512" src="/images/blog/3845814.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1458" style="width:69px;height:auto"  sizes="auto, (max-width: 512px) 100vw, 512px" /></figure>



<figure class="wp-block-audio"><audio controls src="/images/blog/5-PHP-Errors-You-Must-Know.wav"></audio><figcaption class="wp-element-caption"><strong>Listen to the Podcast Version of This Blog</strong></figcaption></figure>



<div style="height:33px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>2. Logical Errors:</strong></p>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Description:</strong> Rather&nbsp;of&nbsp;producing&nbsp;a&nbsp;syntax&nbsp;error,&nbsp;these&nbsp;mistakes&nbsp;cause&nbsp;unexpected&nbsp;behavior&nbsp;or&nbsp;inaccurate&nbsp;program&nbsp;output.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>How&nbsp;to&nbsp;Address&nbsp;It:</strong> Print&nbsp;statements,&nbsp;logging,&nbsp;and&nbsp;step-by-step&nbsp;code&nbsp;execution&nbsp;are&nbsp;examples&nbsp;of&nbsp;debugging&nbsp;tools&nbsp;that&nbsp;can&nbsp;be&nbsp;used&nbsp;to&nbsp;find&nbsp;and&nbsp;fix&nbsp;logical&nbsp;flaws.</p>
</div>
</div>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="592" height="206" src="/images/blog/syntaxerror.jpg" alt="image of  PHP Errors" class="wp-image-678" style="width:561px;height:auto"  sizes="auto, (max-width: 592px) 100vw, 592px" /></figure>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>3. Runtime Errors:</strong></p>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Description:</strong>Happen&nbsp;as&nbsp;a&nbsp;script&nbsp;is&nbsp;running,&nbsp;usually&nbsp;as&nbsp;a&nbsp;result&nbsp;of&nbsp;problems&nbsp;like&nbsp;division&nbsp;by&nbsp;zero,&nbsp;undefined&nbsp;variables,&nbsp;or&nbsp;improper&nbsp;use&nbsp;of&nbsp;functions.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>How to Solve:</strong> Examine&nbsp;the&nbsp;runtime&nbsp;error&nbsp;message&nbsp;to&nbsp;determine&nbsp;the&nbsp;issue. Runtime&nbsp;errors&nbsp;can&nbsp;be&nbsp;prevented&nbsp;and&nbsp;handled&nbsp;elegantly&nbsp;by&nbsp;using&nbsp;conditional&nbsp;statements&nbsp;and&nbsp;error-handling&nbsp;techniques.</p>
</div>
</div>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>4. Fatal Errors:</strong></p>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Description: </strong>Severe&nbsp;problems,&nbsp;including&nbsp;calling&nbsp;an&nbsp;undefined&nbsp;function&nbsp;or&nbsp;including&nbsp;a&nbsp;file&nbsp;that&nbsp;cannot&nbsp;be&nbsp;found,&nbsp;prevent&nbsp;the&nbsp;script&nbsp;from&nbsp;running. </p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>How to Solve:</strong> Determine&nbsp;the&nbsp;reason&nbsp;of&nbsp;the&nbsp;fatal&nbsp;error&nbsp;and&nbsp;address&nbsp;it. Verify&nbsp;that&nbsp;the&nbsp;necessary&nbsp;files&nbsp;and&nbsp;functions&nbsp;are&nbsp;available&nbsp;and&nbsp;correctly&nbsp;referenced.</p>
</div>
</div>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>4. Notice, Warning, and Errors Messages:</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Description: </strong>Notices,&nbsp;warnings,&nbsp;and&nbsp;errors&nbsp;are&nbsp;sent&nbsp;by&nbsp;PHP&nbsp;to&nbsp;advise&nbsp;users&nbsp;of&nbsp;possible&nbsp;faults&nbsp;or&nbsp;problems.</p>
</div>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Take&nbsp;the&nbsp;following&nbsp;general&nbsp;actions&nbsp;to&nbsp;solve&nbsp;PHP&nbsp;Error:</strong></h3>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Make Error Reporting Active:</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To receive comprehensive error messages, make sure that error reporting is enabled in your development environment.</p>
</div>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Check Logs:</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Examine PHP error logs to learn more details about the errors. The destinations of logs could change based on how your server is configured.</p>
</div>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Employ Debugging Instruments:</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Employ debugging tools like X debug or integrated development environments (IDEs) that support PHP debugging.</p>
</div>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Separate the issue:</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Determine&nbsp;the&nbsp;time&nbsp;and&nbsp;location&nbsp;of&nbsp;the&nbsp;error&nbsp;to&nbsp;help&nbsp;you&nbsp;focus&nbsp;on&nbsp;the&nbsp;problem. To&nbsp;identify&nbsp;the&nbsp;problematic&nbsp;code,&nbsp;use&nbsp;debugging&nbsp;tools&nbsp;or&nbsp;print&nbsp;statements.</p>
</div>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Examine&nbsp;the&nbsp;Documentation:</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For&nbsp;information&nbsp;on&nbsp;error&nbsp;messages&nbsp;and&nbsp;how&nbsp;to&nbsp;fix&nbsp;them,&nbsp;see&nbsp;the&nbsp;PHP&nbsp;documentation.</p>
</div>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Update&nbsp;the&nbsp;version&nbsp;of&nbsp;PHP:</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Make&nbsp;sure&nbsp;the&nbsp;PHP&nbsp;version&nbsp;you&#8217;re&nbsp;running&nbsp;is&nbsp;stable&nbsp;and&nbsp;supported;&nbsp;updates&nbsp;frequently&nbsp;include&nbsp;bug&nbsp;fixes&nbsp;and&nbsp;enhancements.</p>
</div>
