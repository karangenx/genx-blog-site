<p class="wp-block-paragraph">An email account in <a href="https://blog.genxwhosting.com/what-is-cpanel-and-how-is-best-for-blogsite/">cPanel</a> refers to an electronic mailbox associated with your domain that allows you to send, receive, and store emails. cPanel is a web-based control panel often provided by web hosting companies to manage various aspects of your hosting account, including email services.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Login to cPanel:</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Open your web browser.</li>



<li>You can login through the URL: <a href="https://om.genxwhosting.com:2083/">https://om.genxwhosting.com:2083</a>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<a href="https://yourdomain.com:2083/">https://yourdomain.com:2083</a>. in the address bar.</li>



<li>Enter your cPanel username and password.</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">

</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">   2. <strong>Create an Email Account</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>In the Email section, find and click on &#8220;Email Accounts&#8221;.</li>



<li>Access webmail or configure email clients.</li>



<li>Set up email forwarding and autoresponders.</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="1024" height="360" src="https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e2-1-1024x360.jpg" alt="Email Section Image" class="wp-image-537" style="width:653px;height:auto" srcset="https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e2-1-1024x360.jpg 1024w, https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e2-1-300x106.jpg 300w, https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e2-1-768x270.jpg 768w, https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e2-1.jpg 1347w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></figure>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">    3. <strong>Fill in Account Details:</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Choose a username for your email account. This will be the part before the &#8220;@&#8221; symbol, (e.g.&#8221;<a href="mailto:john@example.com">abc@example.com</a>&#8220;).</li>



<li>Select the domain for your email address from the drop-down menu.</li>



<li>Set a protected password for the email account.</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="780" height="606" src="https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e3-1.jpg" alt="Email Details Image" class="wp-image-538" style="width:644px;height:auto" srcset="https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e3-1.jpg 780w, https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e3-1-300x233.jpg 300w, https://blog.genxwhosting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/e3-1-768x597.jpg 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 780px) 100vw, 780px" /></figure>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">   4. <strong>Configure Additional Settings (Optional):</strong></h4>



<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<div class="wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-4fc3f8e1 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex">
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Some cPanel setups may provide additional options such as mailbox quota (storage limit), spam filters, and more. Change these settings according to your tendencies</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">  5. <strong>Create the Account:</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Once you&#8217;ve filled in the necessary information, click on the &#8220;Create Account&#8221; or a similar button to create the email account.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"> 6. <strong>Accessing Email:</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>After creating the email account, you can access your email through webmail (usually accessible via <code>http://yourdomain.com/webmail</code>), or you can set up an email client (like Outlook, Thunderbird, or your mobile device) using the provided configuration details.</li>
</ul>
