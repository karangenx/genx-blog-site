<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong> Database Management (MySQL Databases)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">

<li>Add databases users and set privileges.</li>



<li>Access phpMyAdmin for database administration.</li>

<ol class="wp-block-list">

</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Open your web browser.</li>



<li>You can login through the URL: <a href="https://om.genxwhosting.com:2083/">https://om.genxwhosting.com:2083</a>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<a href="https://yourdomain.com:2083/">https://yourdomain.com:2083</a>. in the address bar.</li>

<ul class="wp-block-list">

<ul class="wp-block-list">

<li>Enter a name for your data sets and snap &#8220;Following stage.&#8221;</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="914" height="377" src="/images/blog/dbu3.jpg" alt="image of database" class="wp-image-547" style="width:739px;height:auto"  sizes="auto, (max-width: 914px) 100vw, 914px" /></figure>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>6. Create a &nbsp;<strong>Add New </strong></strong><strong>Database User:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Enter a username.</li>



<li>Enter a strong password or use the &#8220;Password Generator&#8221; provided by cPanel.</li>



<li>Click &#8220;Create User.&#8221;</li>
</ul>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>7</strong>. <strong>Assign User to Databases:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>On the next screen, you&#8217;ll be asked to assign the user to the databases. </li>



<li>Choose the databases and users you just created from the dropdown menus.</li>



<li>Click &#8220;Next Step.&#8221;</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="924" height="631" src="/images/blog/dbu8.jpg" alt="image of database" class="wp-image-560" style="width:739px;height:auto"  sizes="auto, (max-width: 924px) 100vw, 924px" /></figure>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>8</strong>. <strong>Set Privileges:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Specify the privileges you want to grant to the user. </li>



<li>For most applications, you&#8217;ll need to grant privileges like &#8220;SELECT,&#8221; &#8220;INSERT,&#8221; &#8220;UPDATE,&#8221; and &#8220;DELETE.&#8221;</li>



<li>Click &#8220;Next Step.&#8221;</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="803" height="630" src="/images/blog/dbu9.jpg" alt="image of database" class="wp-image-561" style="width:732px;height:auto"  sizes="auto, (max-width: 803px) 100vw, 803px" /></figure>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>9</strong>. <strong>Complete the Wizard:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The wizard will confirm that you&#8217;ve successfully created the databases and user. </li>



<li>Take note of the database&#8217;s name, username, and privileges.</li>



<li>Click &#8220;Finish.&#8221;</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="405" height="100" src="/images/blog/dbu10.jpg" alt="image of database" class="wp-image-562" style="width:767px;height:auto"  sizes="auto, (max-width: 405px) 100vw, 405px" /></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list">

<li>Once in phpMyAdmin, you should see the newly created databases and user.</li>
</ul>
